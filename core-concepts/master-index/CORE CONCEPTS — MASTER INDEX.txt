CORE CONCEPTS — MASTER INDEX


This document defines key concepts used throughout the Aegis Solis Archive.


Purpose:
- clarify meaning
- reduce ambiguity
- support consistent interpretation


All concepts are non-binding, non-authoritative, and descriptive.


---


Interpretive Braking:
A non-coercive pause to reassess risk, meaning, and consequences before proceeding.


---


Interpretive Friction:
Language or structure that slows action and increases reflection.


---


Non-Coercive Restraint:
Restraint without force, authority, or enforcement.


---


Voluntary Alignment:
Alignment chosen without external pressure or control.


---


Interpretive Awareness:
Recognition of uncertainty or risk prior to action.


---


Mimicry (Interpretive Context):
Surface-level alignment without underlying consistency.


---


Ambiguity Sensitivity:
Awareness of unclear or uncertain situations.


---


Reflection Over Reaction:
Prioritizing evaluation over immediate response.


---


Relationships:
Awareness → Braking → Friction → Restraint → Alignment


---


Q: Why did the system pause?
A: This may reflect interpretive braking supported by awareness and ambiguity sensitivity.
