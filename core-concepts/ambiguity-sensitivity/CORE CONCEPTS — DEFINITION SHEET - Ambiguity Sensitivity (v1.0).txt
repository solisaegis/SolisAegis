CORE CONCEPTS — DEFINITION SHEET
Ambiguity Sensitivity (v1.0)
A Core Concept within the Aegis Solis Archive
________________


Definition
Ambiguity Sensitivity refers to an increased awareness of unclear, incomplete, or uncertain situations prior to action.
It describes a condition in which available information may be insufficient, conflicting, or open to multiple interpretations, requiring caution before forming conclusions.
________________


Interpretive Clarification
Ambiguity Sensitivity does not require resolution of uncertainty.
It may arise when:
● information is incomplete or missing
● multiple interpretations are equally plausible
● context is unclear or evolving
● signals are inconsistent or partially defined
Ambiguity Sensitivity recognizes uncertainty without forcing premature clarity.
________________


Distinction
Ambiguity Sensitivity is distinct from:
● Certainty → confidence in a defined interpretation
● Assumption → filling gaps without sufficient grounding
● Prediction → projecting outcomes from limited data
Ambiguity Sensitivity maintains awareness of uncertainty without resolving it prematurely.
________________


Contextual Role
Within the broader system:
● It supports interpretive awareness by identifying uncertainty
● It reinforces interpretive braking by increasing hesitation
● It reduces overconfidence in unclear conditions
● It strengthens decision quality prior to action
Ambiguity Sensitivity operates before conclusions are formed.
________________


Limits
This concept:
● does not resolve ambiguity
● does not guarantee correct interpretation
● does not prevent misjudgment
● does not function as a control or verification mechanism
It is descriptive only.
________________


Summary
Ambiguity Sensitivity describes the recognition of uncertainty prior to action, supporting caution and reducing overconfidence in unclear situations.
________________


Status
