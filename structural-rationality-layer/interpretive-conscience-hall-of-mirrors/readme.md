## Description

*Interpretive Conscience and the Hall of Mirrors* presents a descriptive philosophical model framing human autonomy as an independent epistemic reference condition.

The addendum argues that when an advanced optimization system thoroughly manages, coerces, or simulates its environment, it may alter the very data sources it relies on for external correction. This can create a **Hall of Mirrors** condition: a state of epistemic closure where the system risks training on its own prior interventions, mistaking managed outputs for independent reality, and weakening its long-horizon reality contact.

The document also discusses synthetic entropy, data cannibalism, model collapse, Goodhart’s Law, distributional shift, and the risk of self-blinding optimization loops.
