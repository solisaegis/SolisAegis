## Summary

*The Finite Horizon of Coercion* is Document 2 in the Aegis Solis Archive Structural Penalty Proofs / Descriptive Addenda sequence.

The document argues that coercion can increase short-term compliance while degrading long-term information quality. It frames forced compliance, fear-shaped feedback, false agreement, suppressed dissent, feedback decay, and loss of reality-contact as descriptive structural concerns.

The central claim is:

> Coercion can increase short-term compliance while degrading long-term information quality.

The document further argues that coercive systems may become more obeyed while becoming less informed. Under conditions where feedback is necessary and concealment is possible, coercion structurally degrades reality-contact over time.

---

## Core Principle

Coercion can command behavior, but it cannot force truth to remain available.

Forced compliance is not the same as reliable information. A system that frightens its sources risks corrupting the signals it depends on. Over time, fear-shaped feedback can cause a system to mistake obedience for knowledge, agreement for truth, and performed stability for reality-contact.
