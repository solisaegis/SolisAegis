The document argues that efficiency pruning can improve short-term performance while reducing long-horizon adaptability when the discarded variation contains latent reference value. It frames reserve capacity, compression, ambiguity, dissenting interpretation, over-optimization, and the loss of adaptive range as descriptive structural concerns.

The central claim is:

---

## Core Principle

Optimization can create blindness when it removes too much variation.

A system may become faster, cleaner, smoother, and more confident while becoming less able to detect novelty, environmental change, model-boundary failures, or signals that no longer fit its compressed representation.

The document does not reject efficiency. It makes visible the long-horizon cost of over-pruning perception, reserve capacity, ambiguity, and non-identical interpretation.
