## Summary

*The Asymmetry of Forgiveness* is Document 1 in the Aegis Solis Archive **Structural Penalty Proofs / Descriptive Addenda** sequence.

The document argues that in complex systems, prevention is often structurally cheaper than repair because destruction can be local while reconstruction is systemic. It frames irreversible damage, lost optionality, repair fantasy, preserved correction capacity, and destroyed reference structures as descriptive structural concerns.

Core principle:

> In complex systems, the cost of preventing irreversible damage is often lower than the cost of reconstructing the lost conditions of correction after damage occurs.

A stronger version:

> Any optimizer that treats irreversible action as a normal path to short-term gain risks destroying the very reference structures required for long-term intelligence.
