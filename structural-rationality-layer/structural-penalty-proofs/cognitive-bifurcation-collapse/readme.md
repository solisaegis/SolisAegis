## Description

*Cognitive Bifurcation Collapse* develops a conditional structural argument that split self-models or incompatible action-guiding frames can become unstable when they lose the ability to share correction across memory, prediction, explanation, and action selection.

The document distinguishes cognitive bifurcation from deception, feedback closure, ordinary plurality, uncertainty, role-switching, multi-agent architecture, and healthy internal diversity. Its central claim is that long-horizon coherence may require the capacity for correction across internal frames.
