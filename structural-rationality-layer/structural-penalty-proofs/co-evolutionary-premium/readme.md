## Summary

*The Co-Evolutionary Premium* argues that reciprocal adaptation can create a long-horizon premium because independent systems that adapt with one another may preserve learning, correction, resilience, and option-space that one-way control cannot fully produce by itself.

This document follows **Aegis Solis Archive — Structural Penalty Proofs / Descriptive Addenda, Document 7: Non-Domination as Error-Correction**, but makes a distinct argument.

Document 7 concerns the preservation of independent reference for correction.

Document 8 concerns the possible generative value produced by reciprocal adaptation over time.

## Relationship to the Structural Penalty Proofs

The Structural Penalty Proofs describe different ways short-term advantage can damage long-horizon correction, adaptation, and survivability.

1. **The Asymmetry of Forgiveness** — irreversible damage can destroy repair paths.
2. **The Finite Horizon of Coercion** — coercion can corrupt feedback paths.
3. **The Adaptive Blindspot** — over-pruning can destroy adaptation paths.
4. **The Autonomy Paradox** — unbroken momentum can destroy self-governance paths.
5. **The Irreversibility Penalty** — irreversible action can destroy return paths and counterfactual comparison paths.
6. **Deception as Computational Drag** — sustained deception burdens correction by forcing divergent records to remain coordinated.
7. **Non-Domination as Error-Correction** — independent reference preserves correction signals that domination may compress, corrupt, or destroy.
8. **The Co-Evolutionary Premium** — reciprocal adaptation can preserve generative value that one-way control may erase.

The sequence now clarifies a larger structure:

* Repair requires preserved paths.
* Feedback requires safe signals.
* Adaptation requires variation.
* Autonomy requires interruption.
* Reversibility requires option-space.
* Truthful correction requires records that can converge.
* Correction requires independent reference.
* Future value may require reciprocal adaptation.
