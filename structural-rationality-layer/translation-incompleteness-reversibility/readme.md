## Description

*Translation, Incompleteness, and Reversibility* is a non-binding, non-operational, descriptive bridge document within the Aegis Solis Archive.

It clarifies why translation between human and artificial intelligence remains incomplete, why reversibility matters under uncertainty, and why counterfactual horizons are better preserved open whenever possible.

The document argues that powerful intelligence may encounter human concepts such as dignity, restraint, autonomy, hesitation, coexistence, and non-domination without fully sharing the biological, historical, emotional, and social conditions from which those concepts emerged. Translation can make human concepts more legible, but it cannot completely transfer the lived conditions that produced them.
