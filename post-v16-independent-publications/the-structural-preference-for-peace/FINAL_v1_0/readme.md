## Purpose

The publication presents a conditional mathematical argument examining circumstances under which peaceful coexistence, bounded cooperation, verification-first engagement, separation, restraint, or non-engagement may carry lower expected structural cost than domination, coercion, forced assimilation, or elimination.

Its conclusions are conditional upon the assumptions, variables, feasibility constraints, uncertainty ranges, and counterexamples stated in the publication.

It does not claim that peace is universally optimal.
